Māui And The Giant Fish

In the Māori myth of Maui and the giant fish, the demigod Maui, using a magic fishhook, pulls a giant fish from the sea, which becomes the North Island of New Zealand (Te Ika-a-Māui), while his canoe becomes the South Island (Te Waka-a-Māui).

Sina And The Eel

"Sina and the Eel" is a Samoan myth explaining the origin of the coconut tree, where a beautiful girl, Sina, befriends a small eel, who grows large and falls in love with her, leading to a tragic end and the emergence of the first coconut tree from the eel's burial.

A Legend Of Degei The Snake God

In Fijian mythology, Degei, the supreme snake god, is the creator of the world, fruits, and humans, and judges souls after death, sending some to paradise and others to be punished or rewarded in a lake. He is also connected to earthquakes, storms, and seasons, and is revered as a god of both creation and wrath.

The Rainbow Serpent

In Aboriginal Australian mythology, the Rainbow Serpent is a powerful, life-giving deity, a creator and protector of water, and a figure of both creation and destruction, often depicted as a snake with a rainbow-like body.
